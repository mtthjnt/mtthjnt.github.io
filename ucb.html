Chasing kids absence seizures

Context
I worked with UCB Pharma and Tim, founder of a startup that aims at helping kids suffering from absence seizures. Tim's original idea consists in using EEG headset to reveal ‚Äúblanking out‚Äù brain activity during classroom.
My involvement in this project was to facilitate a workshop with a focus on the storytelling of Tim's idea and their benefits.
Prototyping the workshop
To understand how I would facilitate the workshop, I have found the need to prototype it first. To put things into perspective, Tim needed to be placed in a role of a supervisor instead of involving him in a team. Like this, he was able to infuse all his knowledge without skewing the participant ideas.
Visualising the series of exercices helps to forecast the different situations
The workshop
Wear the shoes of personas
Personas are personified¬†aggregated descriptions of users. They help to understand typical life stories and lead to the design of services that are more targeted to the intended users. We used 3 personas during the workshop: Julie The Kid, Oscar The Teacher and Delphine The Mother.
Journeys mapping
Taking a detailed look at all the steps and touchpoints that are part of a service experience makes it easy to identify pain points, problems, and issues in the journey. Once identified, these can be isolated and eliminated, or fixed to take friction out of the process. It is also easy to identify points of satisfaction; these can be extended or replicated to provide a more positive service experience.
The "1h classroom" journey of Julie The Kid
