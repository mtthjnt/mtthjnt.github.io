Old Mutual

Towards a better retirement system

Context
I conducted a usability analysis for Old Mutual, an international financial services provider based in South Africa. The analysis scope was three of their corporate portals that tied up 3 kind of users around retirement solutions: Old Mutual’s client HR people, Old Mutual’s clients employees, and brokers.
While browsing the portals and meeting stakeholders, we realised that the main issue to solve was more a question of utility rather than a question of usability. Put simply, Old Mutual's corporate business is articulated around retirement solution for employees; mainly blue-collars. The company provides them information only through mobile applications when those employees do not have smartphones or good internet connection. Consequently, when they have questions or requests, the employees used to call their HR managers. As the HR portal doesn't provide enough information (or the right information) to fit all the employees questions/requests, HR people used to call either Old Mutual brokers (for SMEs) or Old Mutual advisors (for large companies). 
The discovery
Mapping users painpoints
To have a holistic view of each experience, the first thing I did was to set up a visual sitemap for each portal. These were a good start to assess the navigation structure and understand primary painpoints such as lack of readability.
The employees visual sitemap shows a very wide navigation 
Listen to user stories
Interviewing by asking simple questions allowed us to understand more about what people are doing and, more importantly, why they are doing what they are doing. 
Content precedes design
With all the insights gathered during the discovery phase we continue the project by prioritising and sketching the adjustments to fix and enhance the user experience of the portals. 
The left post-its show different painpoints. The right ones show early ideation to fix them and a first draft on how to group the content.
Sketching different recommendations
Enhancing the user experience 
From the absence of system feedback to visual inconsistency, the room for improvement was high. Old Mutual could make serious enhancement on the 7 following clusters:
     •  writing and content quality,
     •  information architecture and navigation,
     •  page layout and visual design,
     •  interaction patterns,
     •  trust and credibility,
     •  system performance,
     •  and process.
Users in contexts
Solve the right problem
We were confident in the recommendations we made to improve the usability of the web portals. We were nevertheless convinced that the issue is less a question of user interface design than a question of value proposition for each key stakeholder.
Even by improving the usability, the three portals will remain under used. Moreover, all Old Mutual teams dedicated to the customer relationship (internal consultants, internal brokers, customer advisors) will still be overwhelmed with basic inquiries from brokers, companies and members.
To reduce this workload and reach Old Mutual strategic goals, we suggested Old Mutual to define a new relationship model by putting the users at its heart. By doing this Old Mutual will be able to explore new opportunities, innovate on its market and seize new business opportunities.
At employees' level
With the widespread availability of smartphones across all layers of the South-African society, we are convinced that allowing members to request basic information through instant messaging can substantially improve their experience. Moreover this interaction can be used as a trigger to encourage users to start using more complex functionalities on the website. 
At HR managers' level
HR representatives have a very busy calendar and a lot of daily tasks. The Old Mutual client dashboard should be proactive and help company ease and simplify the administration efforts required to manage the retirement scheme. 
Moreover the dashboard should work as a collaboration tools to serve the satisfaction of their employees. 
At brokers' level
Brokers should easily access information about members they represent and manage. 
System should provide them with a 360 view of members’ profile, displaying all the meaningful information and functionalities that will allow them to give focused advices and act on their behalf.
