BNPP x Carrefour

Retail Mobile Wallet

Context
Advances in mobile technologies and increasing customer expectations have opened the door for new players to offer competitive service experiences in retail markets. In light of this small and large retailers, as well as traditional payments methods are at risk of being left behind.
In this context, I had the opportunity to work with BNP Paribas to create an ecosystem around the shopping experience for customers and merchants in the French retail market.
As a first step BNP Paribas's strategy has been to attract big retail chain companies such as Carrefour. After a 2-year Proof Of Concept development for its mobile wallet, we helped BNP Paribas to design a user-centered response that focus on the value proposition for customers and merchants.
Ground observation
Originally, the project carried the ambition to dematerialise our wallets by embedding them directly on our phones. Starting from this point, we deemed it necessary to look inside different people’s wallets in order to understand the value they attach to certain items.
We visited 3 Carrefour outlets to observe their operations and their customers’ journeys. We also spontaneously interviewed customers on-site to understand their shopping habits. 
Ideas generation workshop
We ran a workshop to align stakeholder opinions and creates as many ideas as possible. It focused on the value proposition for both end-users. Then, participants stepped into the shoes of select personas and dived into extreme scenarios. We focused on the low points of various scenarios and generated ideas to enhance the overall user journey. After voting for the 3 best ideas, we encouraged participants to turn their ideas into tangible prototypes.
“We are bankers so we focused on developing a tool rather than its end use. So far, I feel we made an engine, and today we designed the whole car.” — A workshop participant
Opportunity spaces
We identified 9 idea clusters during the workshop that led us to create opportunity spaces. This allowed the design team to easily explore, understand, and visualise different design directions.
Scenarios
From the stakeholder feedback we illustrated 2 key opportunity spaces (seamless payment and security/storage) through 3 scenarios.
The scenarios were seamless payment for "2 roommates" in one hand and for "a mother and her daughter" in another hand. The third scenario we explored was "the traveller" for security and storage opportunity space.
Iterating a lot and keep track of design work through user flows
We created interactions that function in a standalone application and that can also be integrated as a plug-in solution
The prototype above shows how the solution works as a plugin inside the existing Carrefour app
This prototype shows how the solution lives as a standalone application.
In this example, someone is buying a book at La Fnac.
Usabilty testing
We led evaluations over 2 days to test our different scenarios. One thing that stood out during the user-evaluation was that everyone was concerned with security. We highly recommend BNP to infuse this notion not with more process but more in the perception of security through the interface (icons, feebback messages, user interface style, etc.)
In few words
To introduce the value proposition, Fjord suggests that BNP Paribas build the new service on an existing users base and target "early adopters", such as Carrefour clients. BNPP will therefore provide the payment component as a bank and that will achieve the trust people need to use the service.
Our design principles helped BNP Paribas to also think beyond mobile interface; explaining the fundamental thinking on how to design and implement a holistic service ecosystem.
And finally, extend the value proposition by extracting from a very competitive environment to offer higher added value services such as the dematerialisation of transportation card or the certification of identity.
