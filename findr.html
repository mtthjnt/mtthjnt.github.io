FINDR

Get back to your lost items

Context
With a friend, we had the idea to create a service around lost items. Here was the pitch: 
Today, thousands of personal items are lost every day: keys, telephone, identity papers, handbags, sweater... This represents a huge flux of value, from several million euros per year across France. Much more on a global scale. But today, for lack of efficient service allowing their restitution, the majority of objects are lost forever.

Why?
When you lose an object, you are generally ready to do anything to recover it: because we are often very emotionally attached to this object or because replacing it may cost us a lot (in terms of money or time).

But the problem is on the other side: when you find a lost object, we may have the will to return it to its owner. But, due to the lack of an effective service, we prefer to avoid the pain to return it, by keeping it or getting rid of it.
Value proposition
We want to create a service that facilitates the return of lost items between individuals.
Guiding principles
— 100% online service
— Principle of match between the finder and the "loser": nature of the object, date ofloss, area of ​​loss
— Do everything to make the finder's life easier: just a photo of the object is enough(Metadata)
— Set the return value ourselves: to prevent the service from beingtransforms into a black market, but also to encourage restitution by the "loser".
— Give the possibility to the finders to return the object for free if they wish
— Give the possibility to the "losers" to tip the finder if they want
Business opportunities
Monetize a flux that represents millions of euros and take a commission on it, for intermediation.
