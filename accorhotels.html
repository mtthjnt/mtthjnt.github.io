AccorHotels

Employee engagement is power

A tailor-made design approach
We adapted the design sprint methodology to meet the project context: worldwide¬†presence and different brands positioning of the group, multiple profiles working in hotels and temporal constraints.
This approach allowed us to focus our efforts on the most important issues, to quickly make our ideas tangible, put them in the hands of employees, test them and ensure that the imagined concepts meet employees‚Äô expectations in a relevant, useful way.
The heart of the project was structured in 3 design sprints. Each sprint was organized in 2 phases of 1 week each.
A preview from the experimentation plan (briefing the AccorHotels team identify the right profiles)
On the left: Monday, doing research (interview + cards sorting) with the hotel staff (here in Singapore). On the right: Friday, testing the "adoption" of our concepts with different profiles
Concepts
In Singapore, we imagined a service of micro-learning by giving employees the opportunity to create and share short video tutorials.
3 concepts were imagined during the second day. Only one was selected by the client and prototyped for testing adoption on Friday
Short video tutorials from your colleagues (Singapore)
Recording Friday testing. In that project, we tested the "adoption" (rather than the usability) using a "think aloud approach"
In London, we rethought the annual performance meeting by replacing it through structured rituals that happen every 3 months. ¬†
Frequent and structured meeting rituals (London)
In Sao Paulo, we addressed the recognition need by making positive feedback and moments between guests and employees easier.
Moments with the guests and positive feedback (Sao Paulo)
Engage hotels owners with our findings
Delivering our prototypes and findings in an engaging way: using QR code to "augment" the content with interactive elements such as video or clickable prototypes. We used a paper medium as, during our research, we realised that hotels owners used to read hotel industry updates generally in newspapers. The 2 thumbnails (you can click to enlarge) tell our insights from research in the form of "field report".¬†
Providing interaction guidelines to handoff the prototype to a third partner for this project: OCTO Technology
Toward an employee brand
In seeking to understand how employees would use these concepts, we also discovered 7 engagement territories that outline the scope of a new employer brand. An employer brand that will be well known for its ability to provide an attractive work environment for its people and consequently has a positive impact on turnover rate as well as on guest experience.
